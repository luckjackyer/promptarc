Why this prompt works

This prompt anchors AI workflow and process, then adds modular sections, clear hierarchy, icons, and readable structure and practical quality guardrails. It is easy to reuse because the subject can be swapped without losing the composition logic.

Best use cases

This example is best for workflow explainers, educational diagrams, visual guides, and process maps. The main strength here is modular sections, clear hierarchy, icons, and readable structure.

How to adapt it

Start from the information architecture, then add icons, section rhythm, and a readability constraint.

Model and ratio notes

Choose models that preserve modular layout and simple icons. Keep real explanatory text outside the generated image.

Use 3:4 for vertical guides, 4:3 for presentation graphics, and 1:1 for social summaries.

Reusable variables

  • Subject variable: replace the current theme (AI Workflow and Process) with your product, person, event, or scene.
  • Composition variable: keep the quality focus around modular sections, clear hierarchy, icons, and readable structure, then adjust close-up, top-down, centered framing, whitespace, or panels.
  • Output variable: choose 1:1, 4:5, 9:16, or 16:9 based on the publishing channel and specify whether title-safe space is needed.

Common failure checks

  • Keep real instructional text outside the generated image.
  • Check that sections and arrows read in a clear order.
  • Reduce icon density if the layout becomes noisy.

Suggested workflow

  • Copy the English prompt and replace only the subject first.
  • Generate 3-5 candidates and keep the version with the most stable composition.
  • Then tune ratio, crop, and title-safe space for the publishing channel.
